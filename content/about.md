**The DangleBat Chronicles** is a collection of furry art and stories that focus on themes of tragedy, murder, suicide, depression, substance abuse, and mental illness.

A variety of artists of diverse backgrounds and skills have been invited to explore these themes through narrative and visual form.

This is an intentionally disturbing project and is intended for mature audiences.

>There exists an unusual and rare species of bat-like creature.
>
>They are often brown, white, and yellow—but sometimes they aren't.
>
>They typically have wings for arms—but sometimes they don't.
>
>Among these and many other variations, they still resemble one another but are unique in their own beautiful ways.
>
>While these bat-like creatures do have many genuine and fun characteristics – such as enthusiastic hobbies, deeply loving and caring personalities, and an overall good and pure nature – the one thing that they are infamous for, overshadowing everything else, is that they always seem to die by suicide, often without warning or explanation, and usually by hanging.
>
>Due to this negative stereotype, they have been given the nickname "DangleBats."
>
>This term, depending on the context and by whom it's used, can be anything from a jocular term of endearment to a strongly derogatory insult, or even a hate slur. However, this doesn't change the fact that the stereotype is true, and that these creatures simply can't stop killing themselves – for seemingly no reason.
>
>But why does this keep happening?
